Hi everybody,
 
Welcome to the inaugural issue of Consumer Insider! This newsletter is one item that many of you have asked for at different sessions in the past few months. I hope that it helps us share news among our Consumer SG community and float up new ideas.
 
This is the second summer that sees no outbound travel for most people. Still, we are cautiously optimistic that Singapore’s economy is recovering well, as reflected by the latest GDP growth numbers from the government. The second quarter surged by 14.3% (vs last year), even though it contracted by 2% (vs first quarter) due to the heightened alert. Forecast for 2021 is about 6.5% growth.
 
As we monitor our ongoing business performance, we see a steady momentum of the mobile business and a continuing healthy uptake of 5G. We are among the handful of markets that have launched 5G SA mode, the most advanced form of 5G to date globally, and we continue to lead in network performance and coverage. We are working closely with both established technology players as well as start-ups to bring great 5G use cases to our customers. If you have some great ideas or want to introduce us to companies you know that are working on interesting 5G applications, we’d love to hear from you. Do get in touch with me or Terence Lai. 
 
On the Home business, we launched an enhanced 2Gbps broadband plan to cater to households keen to elevate their home-based learning and work-from-home experience. The launch was effective as it was a quick response to Phase 2 (Heightened Alert), which started in mid-May. On the TV front, sporting content continues to be a stronghold for us. To maintain our Pay-TV and content leadership, we have been executing creative and cost-conscious solutions. We partnered with LiveNow to successfully deliver Euro 2020 to customers with the final and semi-finals in Full 4K.
 
We also had an eventful period in the past few months in other parts of the business. Unfortunately, we had an outage that affected the broadband and digital line experience for a small percentage of our customers in June. The Engineering and Customer Care teams did their best to recover service as quickly as possible and through social media, we kept customers informed with status updates on resolutions to the problem. The teams are investigating how we can continue to improve our recovery time and efficacy of ongoing service monitoring, including at the customer end. 
 
Our Customer Care contact centre in Malaysia was hit with 16 Covid cases in the month of May when Malaysia’s Covid situation escalated. The local authorities instructed the shut-down of a couple of operations, which triggered days of emergency measures, including the conduct of PCR tests for all 700 staff and several rounds of thorough sanitisation of the facilities. We closely tracked all staff placed on quarantine during the period to stop further spread of the virus and I am happy to report that our 16 staff have recovered well. This reflects the high standard of measures put in place and the deep focus on our staff’s well-being. During the shut-down period, we had to redirect a large volume of calls, messaging, and orders to Singapore and the Philippines. Many colleagues stepped out of their normal roles or came out of their annual leave and rest days to manage the off-loaded demand. A BIG thank you to them for their support and doing what’s needed to keep up the service level to our customers - truly living out the I CAN Delight Customers belief, despite the very challenging Covid environment.
 
At a time of rampant price competition, the importance of maintaining our leadership in network quality as well as delivering great value and service to our customers cannot be overemphasised. I have earlier mentioned the launch of 5G SA and the enhanced Home network. On the customer experience front, the I CAN programme team has also been working hard, leading several pioneering, cross-team initiatives to improve customer journeys in several major processes, while also designing new ways we can work smarter, turn around faster, and collaborate better as an organisation. We will tell you more about these initiatives in the next issue of Consumer Insider. It’s going to be a very busy and exciting Q2 and Q3!
 
Enjoy the rest of the newsletter!
 

Anna
#FirstOnSingtel5GSA 
Ultra-fast downloads, smoother video streams and calls, better gaming, and more! From 25 June, the public had early access to all these perks with Singapore’s first 5G Standalone (SA) network, launched by Singtel.
 
Singtel also teamed up with our 5G partner, Samsung, for a contest with 5G Power Up Kits up for grabs. Each kit contained a Samsung S21 Ultra 5G with 5G-SA software and other 5G goodies!
 All participants had to do was tell us how 5G would transform how they live, work, and play. For bonus points they could also share their answers as Facebook or Instagram videos. One creative participant even came up with a song about 5G! Catch them on our social media, along with highlights of other winners on our Insta-stories.
